Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if my car has a timing belt or timing chain?

Generally, timing chains are more common on newer vehicles and larger engines, while timing belts are common on many 4-cylinder and some V6 engines. We can tell you instantly based on your year, make, and model when you call. Chains typically last longer but aren't maintenance-free.

Should I replace the water pump with the timing belt?

Absolutely yes. The water pump is driven by or located behind the timing belt on most engines. The labor to access it is the same, so replacing it during the belt job adds only the cost of the part ($50â€“$150). If the water pump fails later, you'd pay the full labor cost again to access it.

What happens if my timing belt breaks while driving?

On an interference engine (most modern cars), the pistons hit the open valves, bending them and potentially damaging pistons and cylinder heads. Repair cost: $3,000â€“$7,000+. On a non-interference engine, the engine simply stops â€” no internal damage, but you're stranded. Either way, preventive replacement is far cheaper.
