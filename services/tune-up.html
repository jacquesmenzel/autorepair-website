Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between a tune-up and an oil change?

An oil change replaces engine oil and the oil filter â€” it's lubrication maintenance. A tune-up replaces ignition and air intake components that affect how the engine runs. Both are essential but serve different purposes. Think of oil changes as keeping the engine lubricated, and tune-ups as keeping it firing properly.

Will a tune-up fix my check engine light?

If the check engine light is caused by misfires (worn plugs/coils) or air intake issues, then yes. However, the check engine light has hundreds of possible triggers. We always diagnose the specific cause before recommending a tune-up as the solution.

How much will a tune-up improve my gas mileage?

Worn spark plugs alone can reduce fuel efficiency by up to 30%. A complete tune-up on a vehicle that's overdue typically improves gas mileage by 4â€“10%. If your plugs are severely worn, the improvement can be even more dramatic.
