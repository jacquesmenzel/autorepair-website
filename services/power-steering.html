Frequently Asked Questions

Can I drive with a power steering leak?

A small leak may allow short-distance driving, but running the pump low on fluid causes rapid, expensive damage. If you notice a leak, check the fluid level frequently and top off as needed â€” but get it repaired soon. If the steering becomes difficult, don't drive.

Why does my power steering whine when I turn?

The most common cause is low fluid level â€” check it first. If fluid is adequate, the pump may be worn, there may be air in the system, or the drive belt may be slipping. We can diagnose the exact cause quickly.

My car has electric power steering â€” can you fix it?

Yes. Electric power steering (EPS) systems use an electric motor instead of a hydraulic pump. We diagnose EPS issues using professional scan tools to read steering angle sensors, torque sensors, and motor control modules. Many EPS problems are electrical (sensors, wiring, fuses) rather than mechanical.
