Pre-Purchase Car Inspection: Don't Buy Without One

You found the perfect used car on Facebook Marketplace. Great price, low miles, clean photos. Should you just buy it? Absolutely not. Not without a pre-purchase inspection.

What a Pre-Purchase Inspection Covers

Engine & Drivetrain

  • Oil and coolant condition
  • Transmission fluid condition
  • Engine noise and performance
  • Exhaust smoke analysis

Brakes & Suspension

  • Brake pad/rotor thickness
  • Shock/strut performance
  • Ball joints and tie rods

Electrical

  • Full OBD-II computer scan
  • Battery and charging system test
  • All lights and accessories

Florida-Specific Checks

  • AC system performance (critical)
  • Undercarriage corrosion from salt air
  • Flood damage markers
  • UV damage to paint and seals

The Cost vs. The Risk

A pre-purchase inspection costs $100-200. The average unexpected repair without one? $1,500-3,000. It's the best insurance you'll ever buy.
